Effects of Climate Change

Most of the causes of climate change is caused by human beings and
this causes harm the living of many people in Ghana. Below are some
of the effect of climate change in Ghana

Some Effects of Climate change in Ghana:

IMPACT ON AGRICULTURE

Climate change is negatively affecting agricultural productivity, with reduced yields, increased competition for resources, and potential displacement of workers. 

INCREASED EXTREME WEATHER EVENTS

Droughts, floods, heat waves, and wild fires are becoming more frequent and severe, causing damage to infrastructure, crops, and livelihoods. .

SEA-LEVEL RISE

Rising sea levels are threatening coastal areas, causing erosion and flooding, impacting coastal communities and infrastructure. 

RISING IN TEMPERATURE

Ghana is projected to become hotter and drier, with temperature increases of 1.0°C to 3.0°C by 2050 and 2.3°C to 5.3°C by the end of the century.  Erratic rainfall patterns are also impacting agriculture and water resources. 

IMPACT ON FOOD SECURITY

Changes in rainfall and temperatures are impacting food production, leading to increased food insecurity and potentially exacerbating rural unemployment. 

IMPACT ON HUMAN HEALTH

Increased heat waves and extreme weather events are contributing to the spread of water, air, and food-borne diseases.
